Classic Apple Pie Recipe

Ingredients Instructions Image
  • Granny Smith apples - 6 large, peeled, cored, and sliced
  • Granulated sugar - 3/4 cup
  • Brown sugar - 1/4 cup, packed
  • All-purpose flour - 2 tbsp
  • Lemon juice - 1 tbsp
  • Ground cinnamon - 1 tsp
  • Ground nutmeg - 1/4 tsp
  • Butter - 2 tbsp, cold and diced
  • Pastry for double-crust pie - store-bought or homemade
  • Milk or cream - for brushing the crust
  • Granulated sugar - for sprinkling on top
  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine sliced apples, granulated sugar, brown sugar, flour, lemon juice, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Mix well to coat apples evenly.
  3. Roll out half of the pastry and line a 9-inch pie dish. Trim excess dough from edges.
  4. Spoon the apple filling into the pastry-lined pie dish, mounding apples slightly in the center. Dot with diced cold butter.
  5. Roll out the remaining pastry and place over the filling. Trim excess dough and crimp edges to seal. Cut a few small slits in the top crust to vent steam.
  6. Brush the top crust with milk or cream and sprinkle with granulated sugar.
  7. Place the pie on a baking sheet to catch any drips. Bake in preheated oven for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.
  8. Remove from oven and let cool on a wire rack before serving.
