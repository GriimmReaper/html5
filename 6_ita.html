Panissa Recipe

Ingredients Instructions Image
  • Chickpea flour - 1 cup
  • Water - 3 cups
  • Olive oil - 2 tablespoons
  • Garlic - 2 cloves, minced
  • Fresh rosemary - 1 tablespoon, chopped
  • Salt - to taste
  • Black pepper - to taste
  • Optional: 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • For serving: Olive oil for drizzling, fresh rosemary for garnish
  1. In a medium saucepan, bring the water to a boil. Gradually whisk in the chickpea flour, making sure there are no lumps.
  2. Reduce the heat to low and cook the mixture, stirring frequently, until it thickens and begins to pull away from the sides of the pan, about 10-15 minutes.
  3. Stir in the minced garlic, chopped rosemary, salt, and black pepper. Cook for another 2-3 minutes until the garlic is fragrant.
  4. Pour the mixture into a greased baking dish or a sheet pan, spreading it out evenly. Let it cool to room temperature, then refrigerate until firm, at least 2 hours or overnight.
  5. Once firm, cut the panissa into slices or squares. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat and fry the panissa pieces until golden and crispy on both sides, about 3-4 minutes per side.
  6. Serve warm, drizzled with olive oil and garnished with fresh rosemary if desired.
